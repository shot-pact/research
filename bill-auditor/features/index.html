shot·pact · research · feature list

A phone app that finds errors on your medical bills.

shot-pact is a mobile app for people who just got a confusing hospital bill. Snap a photo of the bill (or upload the PDF), the app reads every line, circles the charges that look wrong, explains why in plain English, and writes a dispute letter to the hospital for you. You only pay if you actually get money back.

Capturing the bill

The app accepts bills three ways so you don't have to fight the input.

Camera mode
Point your phone at the paper bill. Four white corner brackets appear on screen, like a passport photo guide. Line the bill up inside them and tap the shutter.
Helper pill at the bottom reads Align your bill within the frame and nudges you if it's crooked.
Photo library
Already took a picture of the bill last week? Tap the gallery icon, pick it from your camera roll.
PDF upload
Most hospital bills arrive as PDFs in email. Tap the PDF icon and attach the file. Multiple pages are fine.
Works for the itemized bill (the hospital's receipt) and the EOB (the "explanation of benefits" your insurance mails you).
03

How the scan works

Once you capture the bill, the app runs six checks in order. Each one takes a second or two. A checklist shows progress so you can see what it's doing instead of staring at a spinner.

STAGE 01
Read the text off the photo (OCR)
Turn pixels into actual characters the app can work with. "99285 ER visit $2,840" becomes real text.
STAGE 02
Check every billing code
Every US medical charge has a 5-character code (CPT or HCPCS). The app checks each code is real and matches its description.
STAGE 03
Look for duplicate charges
Did the same procedure get billed twice? The app spots it by comparing codes, timestamps, and amounts.
STAGE 04
Look for upcoded charges
Did the hospital bill the pricier version of what actually happened? "High complexity ER visit" when the notes describe moderate complexity, for example.
STAGE 05
Check who was in-network
Surgeon was in-network but the anesthesiologist wasn't? That's a surprise bill. There's a federal law against it (No Surprises Act) and the app catches it.
STAGE 06
Draft the dispute letter
Once every error is known, the app writes the letter for you so you don't start from a blank page.
04

The 5 error types we catch

Every flag in the app is one of these five. Each one has a tint (ember = most serious, amber = softer), a severity (high / medium / low), and a confidence score so you know how sure we are.

Error type Tint What it means in plain English Real example from a demo bill
Duplicate ember The exact same charge shows up twice on your bill. Almost always a billing mistake. An ER visit billed at $2,840, then the same code billed again for $2,840 a few minutes later. Second one is recoverable.
Upcode ember You got the basic version but the hospital billed for the fancy version. Costs more, worth fighting. Billed as high complexity ER visit ($2,840) when chart notes describe moderate complexity ($1,420). Difference is $710 recoverable.
Out-of-network ember Your hospital was in-network, but one provider there wasn't. Federal law says they can't send you a surprise bill for it. Anesthesiologist billed out-of-network at an in-network hospital for $2,150. Protected under the No Surprises Act, fully recoverable.
Unbundled amber One procedure got broken into smaller pieces, each billed separately. The pieces should've been included in the main charge. Post-op follow-up visit billed separately for $320. Should have been included in the surgery fee for free.
Opaque amber A giant lump-sum charge with no breakdown. You can't tell what you actually paid for, so it's worth asking. "Supplies & miscellaneous" for $684 with zero detail. Asking for an itemized breakdown usually knocks 15 to 40% off.

Results screen

What you see after the scan finishes. Designed so you know in 3 seconds whether it's worth your time.

Big number at the top
A single headline: "We think you may recover $X". No scrolling, no thinking, just the answer.
For the demo ER bill: $5,786 with 6 of 10 lines flagged, 3 of them high severity.
Stacked recovery bar
One horizontal bar that shows which error types add up to the biggest recovery. Legend underneath labels each segment.
The demo bill's bar: duplicates $2,883, out-of-network $1,890, upcodes $710, opaque $205, unbundled $98.
Three tabs: Flagged / All lines / Clean
Default tab is Flagged since that's what matters. Each tab has a count next to it. You can always switch to see every line or just the boring ones.
Per-line rows
One row per charge on the bill. Shows the code, what it was for, quantity, billed amount, and a red tag if there's a problem. Tap any row to dig in.
"Looks clean" section below
Charges that seem fine show up greyed out at the bottom of the list. Nothing is hidden. That openness builds trust.
Sticky bottom action
A dark pill floats at the bottom at all times reading "Next step: Generate dispute letter". One tap takes you to the letter draft.

07

Dispute letter

The core value surface. The app has already drafted the letter, you just tune it and send.

Check off the claims you want to dispute
A checklist at the top of every flagged charge with a checkbox next to it. Uncheck anything you don't want to fight over. The "Disputing $X" total at the bottom updates live.
You might uncheck the $98 unbundled charge because it's not worth the hassle, but keep the $2,840 duplicate and the $2,150 surprise bill.
Every piece of text is tap-to-edit
Dotted underlines tell you what's editable. Tap the signer, the recipient, the department, or the closing paragraph and you can rewrite it.
Change the signer from "Jordan M. Reyes" to your actual name. Soften the closing paragraph if you want to sound less combative.
Legal citations added automatically
When your bill has a surprise out-of-network charge, the letter automatically cites the No Surprises Act (45 CFR § 149). You don't have to know the law exists.
Per-claim paragraphs
The body of the letter lists each disputed charge as §1, §2, §3, and so on. Each one has the flag name, what it's for, the code, the reason, and the dollar amount.
Pick how to send it
Three delivery options, each with trade-offs.
Certified mail costs $7.82, comes with a postmark receipt, and is the recommended option because the hospital can't pretend they didn't get it. Patient portal uploads the letter as a PDF and costs nothing. Email goes straight to the billing department inbox.
PDF preview
Tap Preview to see exactly what the hospital will receive. Looks like a real legal letter on cream paper, with the shot·pact footer marking who prepared it.

12

Privacy & trust

Medical bills contain sensitive info. These decisions are meant to make people trust the app enough to upload one.

Scanning happens on your phone
Your bill photo never goes to our servers. The OCR and pattern matching runs on-device, so your medical info stays with you.
The only time data leaves your phone is if YOU export a dispute letter to send.
HIPAA-aligned processing
Built to the same privacy standards hospitals follow for your health records. Storage, encryption, and data handling all follow HIPAA norms.
A human reviews every letter
Before any dispute letter goes out, a real person on our team looks it over. That keeps us from embarrassing you with a silly mistake.
Native iOS feel
Uses the iOS 26 "Liquid Glass" look, so the app feels like part of your phone rather than a website dressed up as an app.
Native Android feel
Uses Material Design components, so it feels right at home next to Gmail and Google Calendar.
Not medical or legal advice
This is important and it's in the app everywhere. shot-pact helps you with paperwork. We're not doctors and we're not lawyers. "We flag. You verify."
